Single Malt Scotch
Production100% malted barley, single distillery, pot stills. Aged min 3 years in oak casks.
RegionsSpeyside — fruity, honeyed (Glenfiddich, Macallan). Islay — peaty, smoky, coastal (Lagavulin, Laphroaig). Highlands — diverse, heathery (Dalmore, Glenmorangie). Lowlands — light, floral (Auchentoshan).
TastingVaries widely by region — fruit, honey, vanilla, peat, smoke, dried fruit, oak spice.
ServeNeat or with a few drops of water. Glencairn glass. Room temp or slight chill.

Blended Scotch
ProductionBlend of single malt + grain whiskies from multiple distilleries. Consistent, approachable.
Key brandsJohnnie Walker, Chivas Regal, Dewar's, Ballantine's, Famous Grouse.
TastingLight to medium body. Vanilla, caramel, dried fruit, light smoke. Easy-drinking.
ServeNeat, on the rocks, or with water. Very mixable.

Blended Malt Scotch
ProductionBlend of single malts from multiple distilleries. No grain whisky. More complex than blended.
Key brandsMonkey Shoulder, Johnnie Walker Green Label, Compass Box.
TastingRicher than blended Scotch. Fruity, malty, honeyed with regional character of constituent malts.
ServeNeat, on the rocks, or in cocktails. Great value entry to malt whisky.

Single Grain Scotch
ProductionMade from grains other than malted barley (wheat, corn) at a single distillery. Column still.
Key brandsHaig Club, Girvan, Cameronbridge.
TastingLighter, sweeter than malt. Vanilla, toffee, coconut, light oak. Smooth and approachable.
ServeNeat, on the rocks, or with a mixer. Often underrated.

Straight Bourbon
ProductionMin 51% corn mash. New charred oak barrels only. Distilled to max 80% ABV. No age statement needed if 4+ years.
Key brandsBuffalo Trace, Maker's Mark, Woodford Reserve, Wild Turkey, Four Roses, Knob Creek.
TastingSweet, full-bodied. Vanilla, caramel, corn sweetness, oak, cinnamon, dried fruit. Rich and warming.
ServeNeat, on the rocks, or with a large ice cube. Rocks glass.

Tennessee Whiskey
ProductionLike bourbon but filtered through sugar maple charcoal before aging (Lincoln County Process). Smoother.
Key brandsJack Daniel's, George Dickel, Uncle Nearest.
TastingSmoother than bourbon. Vanilla, caramel, maple, light smoke, banana, charcoal character.
ServeNeat, on the rocks, or with cola. Very popular mixer.

American Rye Whiskey
ProductionMin 51% rye mash. New charred oak barrels. Spicier and drier than bourbon.
Key brandsRittenhouse, Bulleit Rye, WhistlePig, Sazerac Rye, High West.
TastingDry, spicy, bold. Black pepper, caraway, dried fruit, vanilla, mint. Less sweet than bourbon.
ServeNeat or in classic cocktails. The original Manhattan spirit.

Wheat Whiskey
ProductionMin 51% wheat mash. Softer and sweeter than corn or rye dominant whiskies.
Key brandsBernheim Original, Pappy Van Winkle (wheat-heavy bourbon), Maker's Mark (wheat in mash).
TastingSoft, gentle, sweet. Honey, light vanilla, bread, mild fruit. Approachable and easy-drinking.
ServeNeat or on the rocks. Great gateway whiskey.

Japanese Single Malt
ProductionInspired by Scotch methods. 100% malted barley, pot stills. Often uses Mizunara oak (Japanese oak) for unique flavour.
Key brandsYamazaki, Hakushu, Yoichi, Miyagikyo, Ichiro's Malt.
TastingElegant, refined, complex. Stone fruit, honey, sandalwood, incense (Mizunara), subtle smoke, floral.
ServeNeat or Mizuwari (whisky + water + ice). Highball glass or Glencairn.

Japanese Blended Whisky
ProductionBlend of malt and grain whiskies. Japanese distilleries often blend in-house unlike Scotch. Harmony is key.
Key brandsSuntory Toki, Hibiki, Nikka From The Barrel, Kakubin, Nikka Days.
TastingBalanced and harmonious. Floral, honey, light fruit, vanilla, gentle spice. Very approachable.
ServeJapanese Highball — the definitive serve. 1:4 whisky to soda water over ice, stir gently.

Blanco Tequila
AgingUnaged or rested max 60 days in stainless steel. Purest expression of agave.
ColourCrystal clear.
TastingFresh, vibrant, peppery. Cooked agave, citrus, green herbs, white pepper, floral. Most agave-forward.
Key brandsPatrón Silver, Espolòn Blanco, Casamigos Blanco, El Jimador, Olmeca Altos.
ServeNeat (room temp), chilled, or in cocktails. Best for mixing.

Reposado Tequila
Aging2–12 months in oak barrels (often ex-bourbon or ex-wine). Balanced between agave and oak.
ColourLight gold / straw.
TastingAgave + vanilla + light oak. Smoother than Blanco. Cooked agave, caramel, citrus, gentle spice.
Key brandsEspolòn Reposado, Casamigos Reposado, Herradura, Jose Cuervo Tradicional.
ServeNeat, on rocks, or in cocktails. Most versatile expression.

Extra Añejo Tequila
Aging3+ years in oak. Premium, slow-aged, very complex. Category created in 2006.
ColourDark amber, mahogany.
TastingDeep, rich, complex. Dark fruit, chocolate, coffee, leather, vanilla, oak. Minimal agave character remaining.
Key brandsDon Julio 1942, Patrón Extra Añejo, Gran Patrón Burdeos, Fortaleza Still Strength.
ServeAlways neat. Sip slowly. Fine crystal glass. Treat like a premium whisky.

Cristalino Tequila
ProductionAñejo or Extra Añejo that has been charcoal-filtered to remove colour. Retains aged complexity but looks clear.

TastingSmooth, complex, vanilla-forward. Oak character retained but lighter. Best of both worlds.
Key brandsDon Julio 70, Casamigos Cristalino, Maestro Dobel Diamante.

Espadín Mezcal
AgaveAgave angustifolia. Most common mezcal agave (~90% of production). Cultivated. Matures in 7–10 years.
TastingSmoky, earthy, fruity. Roasted agave, citrus, green pepper, smoke, tropical fruit. Entry-level mezcal.
Key brandsDel Maguey Vida, Banhez, Wahaka, El Silencio, Montelobos.
ServeNeat, room temp. Jícara (clay cup) or copita. Accompanied by orange slice + sal de gusano.
Cocktails
Mezcal NegroniMezcal MargaritaOaxacan Old Fashioned
Tobalá Mezcal
AgaveAgave potatorum. Wild agave. Grows in high-altitude forests. Matures in 12–15 years. Rare and prized.
TastingComplex, floral, fruity. Tropical fruit, flowers, herbs, mineral, light smoke, honey. More delicate than Espadín.
Key brandsDel Maguey Tobalá, Los Amantes, Wahaka, Vago.
ServeAlways neat. Sip slowly and appreciate the complexity. Expensive — treat it accordingly.
Cocktails
Neat only recommendedSimple Highball
Tepeztate Mezcal
AgaveAgave marmorata. Wild, grows on rocky cliffs. Matures 25–35 years. One of the rarest mezcals.
TastingWild, funky, herbal, vegetal. Green herbs, fermented fruit, anise, earth, smoke. Challenging and unique.
Key brandsVago Tepeztate, Rey Campero, El Jolgorio.
ServeNeat only. For the adventurous customer who appreciates wild spirits.
Cocktails
Neat only
Cuishe Mezcal
AgaveAgave karwinskii (various sub-types). Semi-wild. Matures 10–20 years. Long, narrow piña.
TastingEarthy, savoury, minerally. Dried herbs, roasted nuts, earth, light smoke, tropical undertones. Structured.
Key brandsVago Cuishe, El Jolgorio Cuishe, Koch El Mezcal.
ServeNeat, room temp. Great for customers curious about non-Espadín mezcals.
Cocktails
Mezcal NegroniMezcal Sour
OriginOaxaca (primarily) + 8 other Mexican states. Can be made from 40+ agave varieties. Ancestral traditions.
Key notesAll mezcal is smoky — agave hearts (piñas) are roasted in earthen pits. "For everything bad, mezcal; for everything good, also mezcal."
Tequila vs MezcalAll tequila is mezcal — but not all mezcal is tequila. Tequila = only Blue Weber agave, specific region. Mezcal = many agave varieties, artisanal production, smoky.

Grain Vodka
BaseWheat or corn. Most common style globally. Clean, neutral, very versatile.
TastingClean, crisp, slightly sweet. Very neutral. Light citrus, faint grain, smooth finish.
Key brandsGrey Goose (France, wheat), Absolut (Sweden, wheat), Ketel One (Netherlands, wheat), Tito's (US, corn).
ServeChilled neat, on rocks, or in cocktails. The go-to mixer vodka.

Potato Vodka
BasePotato. Traditional Polish/Russian style. Fuller-bodied, creamier texture than grain vodka.
TastingRich, creamy, slightly earthy. More body and texture than grain. Subtle sweetness, fuller mouthfeel.
Key brandsLuksusowa (Poland), Chopin Potato (Poland), Woody Creek (US), Chase (UK).
ServeChilled neat — the texture shines. Also great in Martinis.

Grape Vodka
BaseGrapes or wine. Smooth and silky. Popular premium / luxury vodka style.
TastingSoft, silky, subtly floral. Light fruit, gentle sweetness, very smooth. Almost wine-like finish.
Key brandsCîroc (France, snap frost grapes), Guillotine (France), Hangar 1 (US).
ServeChilled neat or in elegant cocktails. Premium sipping vodka.

Rye Vodka
BaseRye grain. Traditional Eastern European style. More character and spice than wheat vodka.
TastingSlightly spicy, dry, bold. Pepper, grain, light anise, clean finish. More personality than grain vodka.
Key brandsBelvedere (Poland, rye), Wyborowa (Poland, rye), Chopin Rye, Żubrówka (bison grass rye).
ServeChilled neat, on ice, or in cocktails. Pairs well with bold mixers.

London Dry Gin
ProductionJuniper-forward. All botanicals distilled together. No artificial flavours added. Dry style. Can be made anywhere.
BotanicalsJuniper (must be dominant), coriander seed, angelica root, citrus peel, cassia bark, and others.
TastingCrisp, dry, juniper-led. Pine, citrus, spice, earthy herbal notes. Clean and classic.
Key brandsTanqueray, Beefeater, Bombay Sapphire, Gordon's, Sipsmith.
ServeG&T with good tonic and a lime wedge. Or straight up in a Martini.

Contemporary / New Western Gin
ProductionJuniper present but not necessarily dominant. Focus on other botanicals — floral, citrus, herbal, fruit. No rules on style.
BotanicalsHighly variable. Cucumber, rose, elderflower, citrus, local herbs, berries, tea, lavender, and more.
TastingVaries widely. Can be floral, fruity, savoury, citrus-forward. More approachable for non-gin drinkers.
Key brandsHendrick's (cucumber, rose), Aviation (lavender, cardamom), Monkey 47, The Botanist (Islay), Empress 1908 (BC, butterfly pea).
ServeG&T with premium tonic, garnish matched to botanicals. Hendrick's = cucumber. Empress = lemon (colour change!).

Old Tom Gin
ProductionSweetened gin — between London Dry and Genever. Historic style, 18th century London. Slight sweetness added.
TastingJuniper-forward with a touch of sweetness. Softer than London Dry. Herbal, lightly sweet, approachable.
Key brandsHayman's Old Tom, Ransom Old Tom, Jensen's Old Tom.
ServeG&T, or in classic pre-Prohibition cocktails where it was the original spirit.

Navy Strength Gin
ProductionMin 57% ABV. Historically supplied to Royal Navy — high proof so if spilled on gunpowder, it would still ignite.
TastingIntense, punchy, bold. All botanical flavours amplified. More juniper, more spice, more everything.
Key brandsPlymouth Navy Strength, Sipsmith V.J.O.P., Perry's Tot, Hayman's Royal Dock.
ServeG&T (the higher ABV still works beautifully with tonic), or in bold cocktails.

Sloe Gin
ProductionGin liqueur (not technically gin). Sloe berries (blackthorn) macerated in gin with sugar. Rich and fruity. Min 25% ABV.
TastingSweet, fruity, tart. Plum, cherry, almond, dark berries, hint of juniper underneath. Rich and warming.
Key brandsSipsmith Sloe Gin, Plymouth Sloe Gin, Gordon's Sloe Gin, Hayman's Sloe Gin.
ServeNeat, on rocks, with tonic or lemonade. Autumn/winter drink. Also great in cocktails.
